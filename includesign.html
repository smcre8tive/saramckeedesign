UX Design — Case Study 01

IncluDesign

An immersive projector game that builds empathy for visual impairment through gameplay — designed for Gen-Z design students.

Role

Product Designer

Team

3 Designers

Duration

3 Weeks

Tools

Figma · FigJam · Canva

Context

SCAD Graduate — 2024

Scroll
01
Background

The Challenge

In a 2024 SCAD graduate studio, my two teammates and I designed an immersive environment product around accessible education. Gen-Z design students value inclusion but lack hands-on ways to practice it.

How might we help Gen-Z design students learn accessible design in engaging ways using immersive environment technology?

of global corporations report D&I commitment — yet only 4% include a disability focus
62%

02
Discovery

Research & Insights

We used secondary research in FigJam to frame the problem space, adopting Microsoft's Inclusive Design framework. A student survey and interview with SCAD's Inclusion Director surfaced four core design constraints.

Microsoft Inclusive Design — a methodology born from digital environments, drawing on the full range of human diversity.
44% of students self-rated inclusive design knowledge at 2/5 — revealing a clear gap in practical application.

"When I think of inclusion, I think of family — ensuring every member engages in the most purposeful way to achieve our goal."

— Director Walker, SCAD Inclusion Director
Empathy-Building
Students need immersive, firsthand ways to understand accessibility challenges.
Practical Application
A gap exists between inclusive design theory and hands-on practice.
Accessible Tools
Learners need financially accessible resources to apply inclusive principles.
Measurable Outcomes
Educators need visibility into student progress on accessibility topics.
03
Process

Ideation & Iteration

Low-fidelity sketches and wireframes drove multiple iterations. We pivoted from a web app with AR scanner to an interactive projector game after user feedback called for a more immersive, shared experience. The web app was repositioned as a controller and accessibility support tool.

Initial sketches: web-app with AR scanner (left) vs. immersive projector game (right).
Post-pivot ecosystem — the web-app became a controller and accessibility layer for the projector experience.
User flow mapping the game sequence and screen transitions.
Mid-fidelity wireframes built on Figma's Apple Oculus design system, adapted for large-format projection.
04
High Fidelity

Visual Design

I owned the login sequence, the spinner mechanic, and the simulation quiz — while teammates handled the design system and remaining game screens.

Login & intro sequence. A hand holding a matching mobile screen simulates the full ecosystem in context.
Simulation spinner — players land on a visual impairment type before the quiz begins, built with Figma prototype animation.
Visual impairment simulation quiz using imagery from the Sight Center of Northwest Ohio. Future iterations would add immersive video and sound.
05
Testing

Prototyping & Testing

We merged our designs into a single high-fidelity Figma prototype and presented to SCAD students and faculty. Feedback was positive on engagement but flagged two gaps: the ecosystem needed clearer explanation, and simulations needed more scenario diversity.

AutoCAD architectural rendering by a teammate — the projector game contextualised in a real university space with diverse users.
Final class presentation — research, design system, and projector game prototype.

Four areas identified for future development:

  • Add immersive video and spatial audio to deepen the simulation.
  • Expand to a wider range of visual impairments and real-world scenarios.
  • Test with diverse users across the accessibility needs spectrum.
  • Source a physical interactive projector for real-world playtesting.
06
Outcomes

Results & Impact

Completed in three weeks, IncluDesign was well-received by peers and faculty. The projector game demonstrated a novel approach to inclusive design education — and deepened my practice in remote collaboration, interaction animation, and accessible prototyping.

Empathy through Experience
The immersive game fostered genuine empathy among design students.
Engagement in Learning
Gamification increased student motivation to explore accessibility.
Future Potential
Highlighted immersive tech as a compelling vehicle for design education.
